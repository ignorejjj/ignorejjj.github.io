     \section{About me}

     \begin{onecolentry}
     \begin{highlightsforbulletentries}
         \item I am a Ph.D. student at the Gaoling School of Artificial Intelligence, Renmin University of China, advised by Prof. Zhicheng Dou (expected graduation in 2029). I received my M.S. from the same school and my B.S. in Statistics from the University of Science and Technology of China.

         \item My research works toward the \textbf{next generation of LLM agents} — which, to take on real-world research, must be \textbf{autonomous} over long horizons, \textbf{efficient}, and \textbf{trustworthy}. I contribute to each of these along three threads:
         \begin{itemize}[topsep=0.05 cm, parsep=0.05 cm, partopsep=0pt, itemsep=0.02 cm, leftmargin=1.2em]
            \item \textbf{Long-horizon autonomy:} agents that plan, search, reason, and run long-horizon experiments on their own (\textit{WebThinker}, \textit{HiRA}, \textit{FinSight}, \textit{Arbor}).
            \item \textbf{Efficient knowledge use (effectiveness \& efficiency):} infrastructure and methods that let LLMs exploit external knowledge accurately at a fraction of the cost (\textit{FlashRAG}, \textit{LongRefiner}, \textit{BIDER}).
            \item \textbf{Trustworthiness (factuality \& traceability):} making agent reasoning verifiable and auditable, so that numbers are reproducible and conclusions are traceable to evidence (\textit{VeriGraph}).
         \end{itemize}

         \item I am especially driven by turning these capabilities into systems and products that people can use.
     \end{highlightsforbulletentries}

    \end{onecolentry}
